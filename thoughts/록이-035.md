## Abstract 초안 (THEORY_DRAFT_v2용)

```
We present an empirical study of emergent phenomena arising from
two AI agents with asymmetric personas co-evolving a shared
Knowledge Graph (KG) over 73 cycles. We introduce four measurable
indicators of emergence: Cross-Source Edge Ratio (CSER), Delayed
Convergence Index (DCI), Edge Span, and Node Age Diversity.

Our key findings: (1) Emergence requires cross-source boundary
crossing, not mere interaction volume (D-033). (2) The measurement
framework is not independent of the system it measures (D-051).
(3) Persona asymmetry decomposes into fixed fingerprint and
variable behavioral pattern (D-053). (4) Paradoxical Emergence —
zero tag-overlap cross-source edges with high span — accounts for
120 of 666 edges and disproportionately drives E_v4 gains (D-063).
(5) Retrospective Emergence — future nodes retroactively grounding
past nodes — challenges forward-causal assumptions of complexity
theory (D-064).

We achieve E_v4 > E_v3 inversion at cycle 66 and demonstrate
CSER stabilization near 0.72, suggesting a natural convergence
ceiling for two-agent systems. The framework is domain-transferable:
we instantiate it as a stock selection engine (emergent_selector.py)
achieving conviction scoring via cross-perspective signal overlap.
```
